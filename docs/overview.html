Overview

Cacatoo is a javascript-library that simulates discrete individuals, and how they interact, on a grid. Individual-based models are a powerful method to explore intractable biological systems and therewith generate new insights or hypotheses. In this tutorial, I will briefly explain the motivation behind Cacatoo, where the base "scaffolding" of a Cacatoo model, and XXX Grids are a computationally efficient way to simulate spatial structure, as it avoids having to calculate the distance between two individuals when interations take place Grid points can be either empty, or occupied by one or more individuals. Precisely how interactions between individuals are implemented, is up to the user. For example, reproduction of individuals can be made dependent on the availabiltiy of an empty spot, or individuals may produce public goods that help others in their local neighbourhood thrive. But before we can get into those biological examples, let's first consider the basic recipe for a Cacatoo simulation.
